Brutalism in architecture meant honesty of materials — concrete left raw, steel left exposed, structure made visible. In web design, the same principle applies: show what the page is made of.

Borders are honest. Drop shadows are a lie.

Borders define territory

Before adding a border, most designers ask: "does this look good?" Brutalism asks a different question: "does this boundary exist?" If the answer is yes, draw it. Two pixels. Black. No radius.

The building should look like what it is — not what we wish it were.

This is not cruelty. It is clarity. When structure is visible, the reader always knows where they are. Nothing is implied. Use border: 2px solid — never border: 1px solid.

Weight does the work

  • Oversized headings establish territory before the reader reads a word
  • Uppercase labels signal classification, not decoration
  • Thick borders separate without softening

Read the full argument in the essay on raw structure. 

:root {
  --color-bg:     #ffffff;
  --color-text:   #000000;
  --color-accent: #ff3300;

  --border: 2px solid #000000;
  --shadow: 4px 4px 0 #000000;
}

The shadow is solid, not blurred. It creates depth the honest way — by describing physical displacement, not light diffusion.
